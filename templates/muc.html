Mini-Urban Challenge

The Mini-Urban Challenge is a national competition sponsored by the United States Air Force Research Laboratory that challenges high school students to design and operate a robotic car to autonomously navigate a model city. The cars are developed using LEGO® MINDSTORMS® Education kits.

The students are challenged to build and program a car that can autonomously navigate itself through a model city. The teams are ranked based on their score: 70% determined by performance on the field and 30% determined by a presentation given by the team of their solution to the challenge.
