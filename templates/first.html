  1. What is FIRST?

    FIRST (For the Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology) is an international organization dedicated to the teaching of the STEM field to students. The organization was founded in 1989 by Dean Kamen and has since grown into one of the largest and most successful robotics high school robotics programs in the world.

  2. FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC)

    The largest competition offered by FIRST, the FIRST Robotics Competition, is an annual competition in which teams are given 6 weeks to build a robot to complete a certain task. The Robots weight approximately 120 pounds and compete head-to-head in teams of 3 during competition. Competitions are generally held on the Regional level, and the year culminates in the final competition held in St. Louis every year.

  3. FIRST Tech Challenge (FTC)

    The FIRST Tech Challenge (FTC) is another robotics competition held annually by FIRST. FTC Robots are much smaller than FRC Robots. They are made from VEX robotics parts. Like the FRC, the FTC game is goal-oriented.
