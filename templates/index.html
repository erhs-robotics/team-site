Eleanor Roosevelt High School Robotics Club

The Eleanor Roosevelt Robotics Club is a group of about 50 bright students from Eleanor Roosevelt High School in Greenbelt, MD who come together to think of, design, and develop creative solutions to a wide variety of challenges in the Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM) Field.

We compete in many Robotics competitions, mainly the FIRST Robotics Competition as Team 53 - "Area 53"

FRC Team 53 - "Area 53"

FIRSTÂ® (For the Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology) is an international organization dedicated to the teaching of the STEM field to students. The organization was founded in 1989 by Dean Kamen and has since grown into one of the largest and most successful high school robotics programs in the world. Click here to learn more about FIRST.

The VEX Robotics Competition is the largest and fastest growing middle and high school robotics program globally with more than 4,800 teams from 20 countries playing in over 300 tournaments worldwide. Events are held often and more are being added every week. This year's game is called Sack Attack. For more information concerning VEX, click here.

The Mini-Urban Challenge is a completely autonomous robotics competition where robots must be built and programmed to self-navigate a model city. Find out more information about MUC here or visit the official website.
